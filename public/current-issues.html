The Future of Natural Farming in India

Author: Khwairakpam Akash

This issue explores the rise of Natural Farming in India as a sustainable, low-cost solution to soil degradation and chemical overuse. Highlighting Zero Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF), we showcase how farmers are reviving traditional practices to heal the land and promote eco-friendly agriculture.

Highlights in this Issue:

Eco-friendly alternative to chemical farming

Natural farming is a sustainable, eco-friendly alternative to chemical farming.

Preserves soil health and boosts biodiversity

It enriches soil naturally and supports diverse farm ecosystems.

Mulching helps retain soil moisture naturally

Mulching conserves moisture and improves soil health naturally.

Supports sustainable agriculture and healthy living

It promotes eco-friendly farming and ensures safer, healthier food.
